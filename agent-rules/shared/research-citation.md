# Research and Citation Rules

Use these rules when notes or plans include researched claims.

- Prefer primary sources: official docs, specs, changelogs, source repositories, or standards.
- Separate verified facts, inferred guidance, and assumptions.
- Include source URLs in durable notes when the claim may need future audit.
- For version-sensitive tooling, record the relevant version or date, for example `Inkdrop v6` or `Mermaid v11`.
- Do not over-quote sources; summarize in your own words and keep quotes short.
- If sources conflict, note the conflict and choose the source closest to the implementation.
